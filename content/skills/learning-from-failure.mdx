FromSoftware games are machines for building bounce-back instinct. There's no emotional padding between death and respawn — just a title card and another attempt.

The implicit lesson: the gap between attempts is precious. What did you observe? What will you change? What assumption was wrong?

In software, this maps to disciplined post-mortems. Not finger-pointing exercises, but structured reviews: what broke, why it broke, and what the system needs to prevent recurrence. Bounce-back is the skill that turns failure from a cost into an investment.
