Taking the shot-caller role in competitive CoD squads was equal parts terrifying and clarifying. You have to make fast decisions with incomplete information, communicate clearly under stress, and own the outcomes.

Minecraft leadership looked different: organizing builds, mediating creative disagreements, setting scope, and knowing when to let someone's vision lead instead of your own.

Leadership isn't rank. It's the willingness to take responsibility for the direction of a group — and to do it with enough calm that others feel safe following.
