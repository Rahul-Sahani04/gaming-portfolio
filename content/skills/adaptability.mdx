Every Call of Duty match starts with a plan that gets destroyed by the first engagement. Adaptability is what separates players who crumble from those who improvise under pressure.

Dying Light forces you to adapt constantly — the world changes at nightfall, resources run out, and safe routes become dangerous. You learn to build fluid contingencies rather than rigid scripts.

In software, this translates to embracing scope changes, pivoting technical approaches mid-project, and treating uncertainty as a design input rather than a threat.
