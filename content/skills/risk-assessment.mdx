Dying Light builds a genuine risk economy: every nighttime expedition offers greater rewards but dramatically higher danger. The game rewards players who calibrate risk accurately and punishes recklessness equally with over-caution.

This instinct translates directly into engineering decisions: deploying untested code on Friday, taking on a feature with unclear requirements, or choosing a new technology stack. Risk sense means understanding the downside *before* committing to the upside.

Good risk assessment isn't about avoiding risk — it's about taking the right ones at the right time.
